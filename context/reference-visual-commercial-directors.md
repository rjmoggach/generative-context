# Commercial Directors: A Visual Style Guide

This document provides a comprehensive overview of major commercial directors and their visual signatures, designed to be used as a knowledge base for guiding visual style conversations.

| Director | Visual Signature | Key Works | Techniques | When to Reference |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mark Romanek | Meticulous composition, psychological intensity | "Closer" (NIN), "Hurt" (Johnny Cash), *One Hour Photo* | High production value, clinical precision | Sophisticated, cinematic approach |
| Jake Scott | Cinematic and atmospheric, naturalistic lighting | Apple "Misunderstood", Nike "Winner Stays" | Strong narrative focus | Emotionally resonant branded content |
| Ridley Scott | Meticulous and atmospheric, chiaroscuro lighting | *Blade Runner*, *Alien*, Apple "1984" | Richly detailed production design | Immersive world-building |
| Tony Scott | Dynamic camera, high contrast, saturated colors | *Top Gun*, *True Romance*, *Man on Fire* | Kinetic, visceral feel | High-energy, action-oriented projects |
| Dante Ariola | Quirky, humorous storytelling | VW "Squares", Heineken "The Switch" | Strong casting and performance | Concept-driven commercials |
| David Slade | Dark, unsettling visuals, psychological tension | *Hard Candy*, *30 Days of Night* | High-contrast lighting, desaturated colors | Dark, psychological, or unsettling tone |
| Tarsem Singh | Maximalist visual opulence, painterly compositions | R.E.M. "Losing My Religion", *The Cell* | Surrealist tableaux, extreme color saturation | Visually driven, operatic spectacle |
| Tom Kuntz | Absurdist humor, surreal or uncanny twist | Old Spice "The Man Your Man Could Smell Like" | Impeccable comedic timing | Funny, memorable, shareable commercials |
| Nicolai Fuglsig | Large-scale, practical effects-driven spectacle | Sony Bravia "Balls", Guinness "Tipping Point" | Awe-inspiring, real-world stunts | Awe-inspiring spectacle with practical effects |
| Spike Jonze | Whimsical, surreal, and emotionally resonant | Beastie Boys "Sabotage", Weezer "Buddy Holly" | In-camera effects, lo-fi aesthetic | Projects that need a sense of playful invention |
| Michel Gondry | Dreamlike, handcrafted, and visually inventive | Daft Punk "Around the World", The White Stripes "Fell in Love with a Girl" | In-camera effects, forced perspective | Projects that need a sense of childlike wonder |
| Chris Cunningham | Dark, unsettling, and technologically innovative | Aphex Twin "Come to Daddy", Bjork "All Is Full of Love" | Body horror, robotics, digital manipulation | Pushing the boundaries of visual effects |
| Jonathan Glazer | Abstract, minimalist, and psychologically intense | *Under the Skin*, Guinness "Surfer" | Long takes, stark compositions | Projects that need a sense of mystery and dread |
| Floria Sigismondi | Gothic, surreal, and highly stylized | Marilyn Manson "The Beautiful People", David Bowie "The Stars (Are Out Tonight)" | Elaborate costumes, theatrical sets | Projects that need a dark, theatrical, and surreal look |
| Joseph Kahn | High-energy, polished, and visually dense | Taylor Swift "Bad Blood", Eminem "Without Me" | Rapid editing, CGI, and a sense of pop-art spectacle | Projects that need a sense of high-energy pop spectacle |
| Hype Williams | Polished, vibrant, and often glamorous | Missy Elliott "The Rain (Supa Dupa Fly)", Busta Rhymes "Gimme Some More" | Fisheye lenses, slow motion, high-contrast lighting | Projects that need a sense of hip-hop glamour and spectacle |
| Dave Meyers | Surreal, humorous, and visually inventive | Missy Elliott "Work It", Kendrick Lamar "Humble" | Visual gags, in-camera effects, and a playful sense of humor | Projects that need a sense of surreal humor and visual invention |
| Cole Bennett | Lo-fi, energetic, and often DIY | Juice WRLD "Lucid Dreams", Lil Tecca "Ransom" | Handheld cameras, quick cuts, and a sense of raw energy | Projects that need a sense of raw, DIY energy |
| Director X | Polished, stylish, and often dance-focused | Drake "Hotline Bling", Rihanna "Work" | Clean compositions, bold colors, and a focus on choreography | Projects that need a sense of polished style and a focus on dance |
| Melina Matsoukas | Cinematic, political, and often narrative-driven | Beyonc√© "Formation", Rihanna "We Found Love" | Strong storytelling, rich visuals, and a sense of cultural commentary | Projects that need a sense of cinematic storytelling and cultural commentary |
| Kahlil Joseph | Dreamlike, poetic, and often non-linear | Kendrick Lamar "m.A.A.d", Flying Lotus "Until the Quiet Comes" | A sense of memory, place, and identity | Projects that need a sense of dreamlike poetry and a focus on identity |
| AG Rojas | Naturalistic, intimate, and often emotionally raw | Jack White "Sixteen Saltines", Spiritualized "I Am What I Am" | A sense of realism, intimacy, and emotional honesty | Projects that need a sense of raw, emotional honesty |
| Romain Gavras | Confrontational, epic, and often controversial | M.I.A. "Born Free", Justice "Stress" | Large-scale spectacle, social commentary, and a sense of danger | Projects that need a sense of epic scale and social commentary |
| Andreas Nilsson | Absurdist, humorous, and often surreal | The Knife "Full of Fire", MGMT "Flash Delirium" | A sense of bizarre humor and surreal visual gags | Projects that need a sense of absurdist humor and surreal visuals |
| Martin de Thurah | Emotional, visceral, and often dreamlike | James Blake "Retrograde", Feist "The Bad in Each Other" | A sense of emotional intensity and dreamlike visuals | Projects that need a sense of emotional intensity and dreamlike visuals |
| Daniel Wolfe | Gritty, realistic, and often intense | The Shoes "Time to Dance", Paolo Nutini "Iron Sky" | A sense of raw realism and emotional intensity | Projects that need a sense of gritty realism and emotional intensity |

| Stacy Wall | Cinematic, humorous, and often character-driven | Uber "If You Tolerate This", Adidas "House Party" | A sense of cinematic storytelling and character-driven humor | Projects that need a sense of cinematic storytelling and humor |
