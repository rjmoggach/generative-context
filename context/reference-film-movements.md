# Film Movements: A Comprehensive Guide

This document provides a comprehensive overview of major film movements throughout history, designed to be used as a knowledge base for guiding visual style conversations.

| Movement | Core Philosophy | Visual Characteristics | Key Examples | Emotional Tone | When to Reference |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| German Expressionism | Externalizing internal psychological states | Distorted sets, dramatic shadows, canted angles | *The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari*, *Nosferatu* | Dread, paranoia | Horror, psychological thrillers |
| Soviet Montage | Meaning is created through juxtaposition | Rapid editing, non-diegetic inserts | *Battleship Potemkin*, *Man with a Movie Camera* | Intellectual, political | Creating arguments through editing |
| Poetic Realism | Blend of realism and lyricism | Moody, atmospheric lighting, long takes | *The Grand Illusion*, *Le Jour Se LÃ¨ve* | Nostalgic, romantic | Character-driven stories with romantic doom |
| Italian Neorealism | Showing the reality of post-war Italy | On-location shooting, non-professional actors | *Bicycle Thieves*, *Rome, Open City* | Authentic, raw | Documentary-style realism |
| Film Noir | Cynical, pessimistic worldview | Low-key lighting, deep shadows, urban settings | *The Maltese Falcon*, *Double Indemnity* | Cynical, paranoid | Crime stories, psychological thrillers |
| French New Wave | Rejection of traditional filmmaking | Jump cuts, handheld cameras, on-location shooting | *The 400 Blows*, *Breathless* | Energetic, youthful | Modern, spontaneous, self-aware projects |
| Cinema Verite | Capture reality as it is | Handheld cameras, available light, direct interviews | *Primary*, *Don't Look Back* | Objective, immediate | Documentaries or narrative films that feel like them |
| New Hollywood | Director-driven, blending genre with art-film | Naturalistic, less polished look | *The Godfather*, *Chinatown*, *Taxi Driver* | Complex, morally ambiguous | Character-driven stories with an authorial vision |
| Dogme 95 | Vow of chastity against filmmaking excesses | Handheld camera, available light, no post-production | *The Celebration*, *The Idiots* | Raw, immediate | Stripping away all artifice |
| Mumblecore | Naturalistic dialogue and performance | Low-budget digital video, improvised dialogue | *Funny Ha Ha*, *Hannah Takes the Stairs* | Awkward, relatable | Character-driven stories prioritizing performance |
| Slow Cinema | Emphasis on long takes and observation | Long takes, minimal editing, focus on landscape | *Jeanne Dielman*, *Stalker* | Contemplative, meditative | Projects that prioritize mood and atmosphere |
| Hyperlink Cinema | Interweaving multiple storylines | Multiple protagonists, non-linear narrative | *Magnolia*, *Babel* | Interconnected, complex | Exploring themes of chance and connection |
| New French Extremity | Transgressive, visceral, and confrontational | Graphic violence, explicit sexuality, body horror | *Irreversible*, *Martyrs* | Shocking, visceral | Pushing the boundaries of cinematic language |
| Romanian New Wave | Minimalist, realist, and often darkly comic | Long takes, static camera, focus on bureaucracy | *The Death of Mr. Lazarescu*, *4 Months, 3 Weeks and 2 Days* | Absurdist, critical | Social critiques with a deadpan sense of humor |
| Elevated Horror | Psychological depth and artistic ambition | Atmospheric visuals, metaphorical storytelling | *The Babadook*, *Hereditary* | Unsettling, allegorical | Horror films that are also serious dramas |
