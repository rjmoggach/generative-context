# Photographers: A Visual Style Guide

This document provides a comprehensive overview of major photographers and their visual signatures, designed to be used as a knowledge base for guiding visual style conversations.

| Photographer | Visual Signature | Key Works | Techniques | When to Reference |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Andreas Gursky | Large-scale, detailed, and often overwhelming | *Rhein II*, *99 Cent II Diptychon* | High vantage point, digital manipulation | Epic scale, overwhelming detail |
| Ed Burtynsky | Large-scale, detailed, and environmentally focused | *Manufactured Landscapes*, *Watermark* | Aerial photographs of industrial landscapes | Exploring the relationship between humanity and the environment |
| Gregory Crewdson | Cinematic, mysterious, and highly staged | *Twilight*, *Beneath the Roses* | Large-scale, elaborately produced photographs | Cinematic, mysterious, and highly staged look |
| Cindy Sherman | Conceptual, performative, and often unsettling | *Untitled Film Stills*, *History Portraits* | Self-portraits in a variety of personas | Exploring themes of identity, gender, and representation |
| Jeff Wall | Cinematic, staged, and often mundane | *Mimic*, *A Sudden Gust of Wind (after Hokusai)* | Large-scale, backlit transparencies | Blending cinematic staging with the appearance of everyday reality |
| Philip-Lorca diCorcia | Cinematic, ambiguous, and often voyeuristic | *Hustlers*, *Heads* | Staged photographs that look like candid street photography | Projects that want to blur the line between reality and fiction |
| Nan Goldin | Raw, intimate, and autobiographical | *The Ballad of Sexual Dependency* | Snapshot aesthetic, available light | Projects that need a sense of raw, unflinching honesty |
| William Eggleston | Democratic, colorful, and often mundane | *William Eggleston's Guide* | Pioneering use of color photography | Finding beauty and mystery in the everyday |
| Stephen Shore | Formal, detailed, and often deadpan | *Uncommon Places*, *American Surfaces* | Large-format photography of the American landscape | Projects that need a sense of formal rigor and detached observation |
| Joel Meyerowitz | Energetic, colorful, and often joyful | *Cape Light*, *Street Photography* | Pioneering use of color street photography | Projects that need a sense of energy, joy, and spontaneity |
| Alex Webb | Complex, layered, and often chaotic | *The Suffering of Light*, *La Calle* | Street photography with multiple subjects and a sense of organized chaos | Projects that need a sense of complex, layered, and chaotic energy |
| Steve McCurry | Vibrant, humanistic, and often iconic | *Afghan Girl*, *Monsoon* | A focus on the human face and a sense of global humanism | Projects that need a sense of vibrant, humanistic, and iconic imagery |
| Annie Leibovitz | Polished, theatrical, and often celebrity-focused | *Vanity Fair* covers, *Rolling Stone* covers | Elaborately staged portraits of celebrities | Projects that need a sense of polished, theatrical, and celebrity-focused imagery |
| Richard Avedon | Minimalist, confrontational, and often psychological | *In the American West*, *The Family* | Stark, white backgrounds and a focus on the subject's face | Projects that need a sense of minimalist, confrontational, and psychological portraiture |
| Irving Penn | Elegant, formal, and often minimalist | *Vogue* covers, *Small Trades* | Studio portraits with a focus on form and composition | Projects that need a sense of elegant, formal, and minimalist portraiture |
| Helmut Newton | Provocative, glamorous, and often controversial | *Vogue* editorials, *White Women* | A focus on female sexuality and power | Projects that need a sense of provocative, glamorous, and controversial imagery |
| Guy Bourdin | Surreal, mysterious, and often unsettling | *Vogue* editorials, Charles Jourdan campaigns | A sense of narrative ambiguity and surreal visual gags | Projects that need a sense of surreal, mysterious, and unsettling imagery |
| David LaChapelle | Hyper-real, colorful, and often satirical | *Vogue* editorials, music videos for Christina Aguilera and Britney Spears | A sense of pop-art spectacle and social commentary | Projects that need a sense of hyper-real, colorful, and satirical imagery |
| Ryan McGinley | Energetic, youthful, and often hedonistic | *The Kids Are Alright*, *I Know Where the Summer Goes* | A sense of spontaneous, youthful energy and freedom | Projects that need a sense of energetic, youthful, and hedonistic imagery |
| Tim Walker | Whimsical, fantastical, and often romantic | *Vogue* editorials, *The Garden of Earthly Delights* | Elaborate sets, fantastical props, and a sense of childlike wonder | Projects that need a sense of whimsical, fantastical, and romantic imagery |
| Nick Knight | Experimental, innovative, and often digital | *SHOWstudio*, campaigns for Alexander McQueen and Dior | A focus on new technologies and a willingness to experiment | Projects that need a sense of experimental, innovative, and digital imagery |
| Pierre et Gilles | Kitschy, colorful, and highly stylized | Portraits of Jean Paul Gaultier, Madonna, and Iggy Pop | Hand-painted photographs that blend religious iconography, pop culture, and homoeroticism | Projects that need a kitschy, colorful, and highly stylized look |
| Thomas Struth | Analytical, detailed, and often monumental | *Museum Photographs*, *New Pictures from Paradise* | Large-scale photographs of museums, cityscapes, and family portraits | Projects that need a sense of analytical precision, monumental scale, and detached observation |
| Hiroshi Sugimoto | Minimalist, conceptual, and often timeless | *Dioramas*, *Theaters*, *Seascapes* | Long-exposure photographs that explore themes of time, memory, and perception | Projects that need a minimalist, conceptual, and timeless look |
| Wolfgang Tillmans | Intimate, eclectic, and often anti-monumental | *Lutz & Alex sitting in the trees*, *The Cock (Kiss)* | A sense of intimacy, curiosity, and a rejection of traditional hierarchies | Projects that need a sense of intimacy, eclecticism, and a rejection of traditional hierarchies |
